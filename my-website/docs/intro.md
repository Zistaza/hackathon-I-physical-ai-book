# Introduction to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

Welcome to the university-level textbook on Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics. This comprehensive resource is designed to provide both undergraduate and graduate students with a deep understanding of how artificial intelligence interacts with the physical world through humanoid robotics systems.

## About This Textbook

This textbook follows a simulation-first pedagogy, emphasizing safety and accessibility by introducing all practical exercises in simulation environments using ROS 2, Gazebo, and Isaac Sim. The content is structured to progressively build your understanding from foundational concepts to advanced applications in humanoid robotics.

## Target Audience

- **Undergraduate Students**: This textbook provides a solid foundation in Physical AI and humanoid robotics concepts, with core content accessible to students with basic programming and mathematics backgrounds.

- **Graduate Students**: Advanced sections and deeper technical content are clearly marked for graduate-level study, providing the depth required for research and advanced applications.

## Textbook Structure

Each chapter in this textbook follows a standardized structure:

1. **Introduction**: Sets the context and explains the relevance of the topic
2. **Learning Objectives**: Clearly defined outcomes you will achieve
3. **Core Concepts**: Theoretical foundations and technical explanations
4. **Simulation Lab**: Hands-on exercises using ROS 2, Gazebo, and Isaac Sim
5. **Assessment**: Questions and exercises to test your understanding
6. **Summary**: Key takeaways and connections to broader concepts

## Prerequisites

To get the most from this textbook, you should have:

- Basic programming skills (preferably in Python or C++)
- Understanding of linear algebra and calculus
- Familiarity with basic robotics concepts (covered in early chapters for review)

## Simulation Environment

All practical exercises in this textbook are designed to work with:

- **ROS 2**: Robot Operating System for communication and control
- **Gazebo**: Physics-based simulation environment
- **Isaac Sim**: Advanced simulation platform for robotics and AI

These tools provide a safe, reproducible environment for learning and experimentation.

## How to Use This Textbook

- Start with the early chapters if you're new to robotics
- Use the simulation labs to reinforce theoretical concepts
- Complete the assessments to test your understanding
- Refer to the glossary for technical term definitions
- Check the references for additional reading and research

## Learning Approach

This textbook emphasizes:

- **Safety-First**: All practical work begins in simulation
- **Reproducibility**: Exercises are designed to produce consistent results
- **Progressive Difficulty**: Content builds from foundational to advanced
- **Practical Application**: Theory is always connected to real-world applications

We hope this textbook provides you with the knowledge and skills needed to contribute to the exciting field of Physical AI and humanoid robotics.
